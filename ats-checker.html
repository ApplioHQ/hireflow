What is an ATS, and why does your resume score matter?

An Applicant Tracking System (ATS) is the software most mid-size and large companies use to collect, parse, and rank resumes before a recruiter reads them. It doesn't automatically reject you, most applications are still reviewed by a person, but a resume that doesn't match the job's keywords, isn't structured in a way the software can parse, or lists duties instead of measurable results ranks lower and is far less likely to be seen.

This free checker scores your resume the way an ATS would, across four dimensions:

1. Keyword match

The single biggest factor. The tool extracts the important skills and terms from the job description and checks which ones appear in your resume, then lists the exact keywords you're missing so you can add the ones that genuinely apply to you.

2. Formatting & parseability

ATS software needs clear contact details, standard section headings, and simple bullet points. The checker verifies your resume is easy for the software to read.

3. Quantified impact

Strong resumes lead with action verbs and back up claims with numbers (percentages, dollars, time saved, scale). The checker measures how many of your bullet points include real, measurable results.

4. Completeness

It confirms your resume has the core sections recruiters and ATS systems expect: summary, experience, education, and skills.

How to make your resume ATS-friendly

  • Mirror the language of the job description, use the exact keywords where they truly apply.
  • Use standard headings: Experience, Education, Skills.
  • Start every bullet point with a strong action verb (Led, Built, Improved, Drove).
  • Quantify your impact: "cut load time 40%," "managed a $2M budget," "grew signups 3×."
  • Keep formatting simple, no tables, text boxes, or images in the body.
  • Include a clear email and phone number at the top.
