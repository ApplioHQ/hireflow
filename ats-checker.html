What is an ATS, and why does your résumé score matter?

An Applicant Tracking System (ATS) is the software almost every company uses to collect and filter résumés before a recruiter ever reads them. Studies suggest that roughly 70% of résumés are screened out at this stage — not because the candidate isn't qualified, but because the résumé doesn't match the job's keywords, isn't structured in a way the software can parse, or lists duties instead of measurable results.

This free checker scores your résumé the way an ATS would, across four dimensions:

1. Keyword match

The single biggest factor. The tool extracts the important skills and terms from the job description and checks which ones appear in your résumé, then lists the exact keywords you're missing so you can add the ones that genuinely apply to you.

2. Formatting & parseability

ATS software needs clear contact details, standard section headings, and simple bullet points. The checker verifies your résumé is easy for the software to read.

3. Quantified impact

Strong résumés lead with action verbs and back up claims with numbers (percentages, dollars, time saved, scale). The checker measures how many of your bullet points include real, measurable results.

4. Completeness

It confirms your résumé has the core sections recruiters and ATS systems expect: summary, experience, education, and skills.

How to make your résumé ATS-friendly

  • Mirror the language of the job description — use the exact keywords where they truly apply.
  • Use standard headings: Experience, Education, Skills.
  • Start every bullet point with a strong action verb (Led, Built, Improved, Drove).
  • Quantify your impact: "cut load time 40%," "managed a $2M budget," "grew signups 3×."
  • Keep formatting simple — no tables, text boxes, or images in the body.
  • Include a clear email and phone number at the top.
