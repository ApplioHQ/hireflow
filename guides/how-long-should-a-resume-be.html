How Long Should a Resume Be?

Updated 2026-07-09 · Applio
For most job seekers, a resume should be one page. Use two pages only if you have roughly 10 or more years of relevant experience or a research-heavy field that requires it. Length should follow substance, never padding.

What is the standard resume length?

The default for the vast majority of candidates is one page. It forces you to prioritize your strongest, most relevant achievements and respects the reality that recruiters often spend only seconds on an initial scan.

A two-page resume is acceptable and sometimes expected once you have extensive experience to justify it. The goal is never to hit a page count. It is to present the most compelling case for the specific role in as little space as needed.

When should you use one page vs. two?

Use one page if you are a student, recent graduate, early-career professional, or have fewer than about 10 years of experience. It is also the safer default when changing careers, since only relevant experience should make the cut.

  • Two pages: 10+ years of relevant experience, senior or management roles, or many quantifiable accomplishments across employers.
  • Three or more pages: Generally reserved for academic CVs, medical, or scientific roles where publications, grants, and presentations are required.

How do you cut a resume down to size?

If you are slightly over one page, tighten before you delete. Small changes usually recover the space you need.

  • Remove roles older than roughly 10-15 years, or condense them to a single line.
  • Cut the objective statement and any generic filler like "references available upon request."
  • Trim each job to 3-5 bullet points focused on results, not routine duties.
  • Reduce margins slightly and use a clean, standard font at a readable size.
  • Delete skills and keywords that do not match the target job.

Does resume length affect ATS performance?

Applicant tracking systems do not penalize a resume simply for being one or two pages. What matters is that the content is parseable, uses standard section headings, and contains the keywords from the job description.

That said, a shorter, focused resume tends to have a higher density of relevant keywords and is easier for a human to skim once it passes the ATS, so concision helps at both stages.

Frequently asked questions

Is a one-page resume too short for a professional?

No. A well-edited one-page resume is standard and often preferred, even for experienced professionals, as long as it captures your most relevant and impressive accomplishments.

Should a resume ever be two full pages?

Yes, if you genuinely have enough relevant experience to fill it, typically 10 or more years. Avoid stretching thin content across two pages just to look more senior.

Does the second page need to be full?

Ideally it should be at least half to two-thirds full. A second page with only a few lines looks unbalanced, so either expand the content meaningfully or condense back to one page.
