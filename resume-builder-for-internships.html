Resume Builder for Internships

Updated 2026-07-10 · Applio
An internship resume is a one-page document that sells your potential over your experience. Because employers know you're early in your career, lead with education, relevant coursework, class projects, and skills from the job posting rather than years of work history.

Why an internship resume is different

Internship recruiters already expect you to have limited work experience, so they screen for potential instead of a long track record. They scan for relevant coursework, class or personal projects, campus involvement, and how closely your resume mirrors the specific internship posting.

Keep it to one page and echo the exact keywords from the job description. Many employers filter with applicant tracking systems, so a resume tailored to each posting beats one generic version sent everywhere.

What to put on an internship resume

  • A short summary aimed at the role, two lines naming the internship and what you bring, so recruiters instantly see a fit.
  • Education with expected graduation date, your major, school, and grad date matter most when work history is thin; add GPA if it's 3.5 or above.
  • Relevant coursework and projects, these stand in for job experience and prove you can already do parts of the work.
  • Transferable skills from any job, a retail or campus job shows reliability, communication, and time management that employers value.
  • Campus involvement and leadership, clubs, teams, and volunteer roles demonstrate initiative and teamwork outside class.
  • Technical and tool skills, list languages, software, and certifications so your resume matches keyword filters.
  • Keywords from each posting, mirror the skills and terms the listing uses to pass ATS screens and signal a tailored application.
  • A clean one-page format, consistent fonts and clear headings keep a busy recruiter focused on your strengths.

Internship resume bullet examples

  • Built a personal finance web app in a semester project, writing 1,200+ lines of Python and demoing it to a class of 40.
  • Trained and scheduled 6 new part-time hires while working weekends at a campus coffee shop, cutting onboarding time in half.
  • Coordinated a club fundraiser that drew 150 attendees and raised $2,300 for a local nonprofit.

Frequently asked questions

How do I write an internship resume with no work experience?

Lead with education, relevant coursework, and class or personal projects, then add campus involvement and any skills. Recruiters expect limited experience for internships, so focus on demonstrating potential and effort.

Should an internship resume include a GPA?

Include your GPA if it's roughly 3.5 or higher, or if the posting requests it. If it's lower, leave it off and let coursework, projects, and skills carry your application.

How long should an internship resume be?

One page. As a student or early-career applicant you rarely need more, and a tight single page is easier for recruiters to scan quickly.
