Resume Builder for College Students

Updated 2026-07-10 · Applio
A student resume flips the usual formula: with little or no work history, you lead with education, projects, and campus involvement instead of jobs. Focus on showing skills through coursework, activities, and measurable results so employers see potential, not just experience.

Why a student resume is different

Most resume advice assumes years of full-time jobs to list. As a college student, you likely have none of that yet, and that's completely normal. Instead of a long work history, your strongest assets are your education, class projects, leadership in clubs, and any part-time or campus work you've done. Employers hiring students and new grads expect this and want to see potential, drive, and transferable skills.

That means the layout shifts: your Education section moves to the top, and you fill the page with projects, coursework, activities, and skills rather than job titles. One page is plenty. The goal is to prove you can learn fast, take initiative, and deliver results, even if you gained those abilities in a classroom or a student org rather than a cubicle.

What to put on a student resume

  • Your degree, major, expected graduation date, and school, so recruiters immediately see where you are in your studies.
  • GPA if it's 3.5 or above, because a strong GPA signals discipline when you lack a work track record.
  • Relevant coursework tied to the role, which shows you have foundational knowledge even without job experience.
  • Academic or personal projects with a one-line description of what you built or analyzed and the outcome.
  • Leadership roles in clubs, teams, or student government, since managing people and events proves real responsibility.
  • Part-time, campus, or summer jobs of any kind, because they demonstrate reliability, time management, and work ethic.
  • Volunteer work and community involvement, which fill out your experience and show initiative and character.
  • Technical, software, and language skills listed concretely, so recruiters can match you to specific tools and requirements.

Student resume bullet examples

  • Led a 5-person team on a semester-long marketing project, delivering a campaign proposal that scored in the top 10% of the class.
  • Managed the front desk at a campus recreation center, processing over 80 member check-ins per shift while resolving scheduling conflicts.
  • Built a Python program to analyze a public dataset of 10,000+ records, producing visualizations for a final course presentation.

Frequently asked questions

What if I have no work experience at all?

That's fine, and very common. Lead with your education, class projects, coursework, clubs, and volunteer work; these show skills and initiative just as effectively as a job.

Should I include my GPA?

Include it if it's 3.5 or higher, or if a specific employer asks for it. If it's lower, leave it off and let your projects and activities do the talking.

How long should a college student's resume be?

One page. Recruiters spend only seconds per resume, so keep it focused on your most relevant education, projects, and skills rather than padding it out.
