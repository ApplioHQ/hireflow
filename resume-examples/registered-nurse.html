Frequently asked questions

What should a registered nurse put at the top of a resume?

Lead with your active RN license (and state), followed by key certifications like BLS and ACLS. Employers and many application systems screen for these credentials first, so make them easy to find.

How do I show clinical experience effectively?

Specify your unit or specialty, typical patient load or ratio, and patient population, then tie your bullets to outcomes like reduced readmissions, improved satisfaction, or strong safety records. Concrete volumes signal the pace and complexity you can handle.

How long should a nursing resume be?

One page works for new grads; experienced RNs can use two pages to cover multiple units, certifications, and clinical rotations. Always include a dedicated licenses and certifications section.
