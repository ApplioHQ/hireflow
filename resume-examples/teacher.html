Teacher resume bullet point examples

  • Designed and delivered standards-aligned lessons for classes of up to X students across multiple subjects.
  • Improved class-average assessment scores by X% through data-driven instruction and targeted interventions.
  • Implemented differentiated instruction and accommodations to support diverse learners and IEP students.
  • Integrated educational technology into lessons, increasing student engagement and participation.
  • Established clear classroom-management routines that reduced behavioral disruptions.
  • Communicated regularly with parents and guardians, strengthening home-school partnerships.
  • Collaborated with grade-level teams to align curriculum and share effective teaching strategies.
  • Mentored a student teacher and supported new-teacher onboarding within the department.

Frequently asked questions

What should a teacher put at the top of a resume?

Lead with your state teaching certification, licensure, and the grade levels and subjects you're endorsed to teach. Districts screen for valid certification first, so make it prominent.

How do I quantify results as a teacher?

Use growth in test scores, attendance, engagement, or the number of students you supported, framed as example ranges. Even non-tested outcomes like improved behavior or successful IEP goals show impact.

How long should a teacher resume be?

One to two pages depending on experience. New teachers should include student teaching and practicum experience; veteran teachers can add professional development, leadership, and certifications.
